The Problem

Cargo changes hands hundreds of times a day. Nobody documents the state at the moment of transfer.

A pallet arrives with a crushed corner. Was it damaged in transit or at your dock? A truck departs with a dent. Was it pre-existing or caused on-site? Without visual proof captured at the exact moment of oversight, the answer is always "it depends on who you believe." Disputes default to whoever has the better lawyer, not whoever is right.

The current process: a photo on someone's phone, if they remember to take it. In practice: paper checklists, clipboard walk-arounds, CCTV footage nobody reviews until something goes wrong, and even then it may be overwritten.

The Solution

Your camera setup. Automated proof.

Oversight connects to any IP camera via RTSP or ONVIF. Draw a zone over the area of interest, choose a task, and go live. From that point on, every oversight event is automatically captured, AI-analyzed, and stored as a timestamped evidence record. No one needs to press a button.

No proprietary hardware. No long installation project. No capital budget conversation. A warehouse manager can configure a dock door camera in an afternoon and have their first evidence report that same day.

📋

Documentation

Captures cargo condition at arrival and departure. Generates a timestamped PDF evidence report with AI-analyzed damage findings, ready to attach to any freight claim.

🔍

OCR

Reads labels, barcodes, QR codes, and BOL numbers from cargo in the zone. Matches against expected values and logs every scan with the corresponding image.

👁

Continuous Inspection

Monitors areas over time: racking condition, floor hazards, compliance zones. Detects changes from baseline and generates weekly condition reports with alerts.

Setup

Live in an afternoon. No installation team required.

1
Connect your camera Enter an RTSP URL or scan the QR code on a new Oversight-bundled camera. Live preview appears immediately. Works with any existing IP camera on your network.
2
Draw your zone Drag a polygon over the area of interest on the live feed: a dock door opening, a vehicle bay, a pallet staging area. One camera can monitor multiple zones.
3
Choose a task and trigger Select Documentation, OCR, or Continuous Inspection. Set the trigger: motion entering the zone, a time schedule, or manual. No further configuration needed.
4
Evidence is automatic from here Every trigger event captures frames, runs AI analysis, and saves a timestamped record to your evidence log. PDF reports generated for flagged events. Retention period configurable.

Vision

The infrastructure layer for liability at every oversight point.

Every time a physical asset changes hands (a pallet onto a truck, a container into a terminal, a vehicle through a gate) there is a liability question. Oversight's goal is to make that question answerable automatically, everywhere, for any operator.

The dock is the wedge. The platform is broader. Oversight is infrastructure for documented custody at any physical oversight: logistics, cold chain, ports, industrial maintenance handovers. The camera is the input. The evidence record is the product.

The moat is not the AI model. It is the evidence record itself: timestamped, camera-verified, legally defensible. Once operators have six months of Oversight evidence in a dispute they win, they do not stop using it.
